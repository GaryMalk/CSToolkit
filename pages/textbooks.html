Textbooks

Free Open Learning Textbooks

  • Open Textbook Library has over 1600 free textbooks, supported by the Open Education Network. in PDF, EPUB, and Online HTML formats.
  • Pressbooks Directory is a free, searchable catalog that includes 8,077 that are easy to copy, revise, remix, and redistribute using Pressbooks' publishing platform.
  • Open Stax run by Rice University is another source of free textbooks using Creative Commons licenses. Most can also be found on the Open Textbook Library.
  • B.C. Open Collection by BC Campus aggregator of textbooks

Open textbooks can also be found on some courseware sites.

Curricula

  • CodeHS has over 100 customizable courses, a built in LMS, and an online IDE that supports 10+ languages including avaScript, Java, HTML, Python, C++, C#, & React Native.
  • BWagner, Computer Science curricula for International Baccalaureate (IB) classes.
  • Canvas Commons is a free service. Content can be downloaded or imported directly into a Canvas course shell by a teacher Canvas account. Educators can upload content, too.
  • Coursera founded by Stanford and Princeton, has free content, paid content, and paid content that can be audited for free.
  • EdX was founded by MIT and Harvard. It is a paid service, but some courses can be audited for free.
  • MIT Open Courseware is a free service containing content from nearly all MIT classes
  • Cubits is a paid server with primarily computer science content with emphasis on Algorithms and AI. Cubits was founded by Princeton Professor Robert Sedgewick and contains mostly his content. Most of the courses can also be found on Coursera.
  • OER Commons is a public digital library of textbooks and courseware
  • Concordia OER directory to search for digital resources
  • The Ontario Curriculum on Computer Studies published by the Ontario Ministry of Education for secondary education.
