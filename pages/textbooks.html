Textbooks

Free Open Learning Textbooks

  • Open Textbook Library has over 1600 free textbooks, supported by the Open Education Network. in PDF, EPUB, and Online HTML formats.
  • Pressbooks Directory is a free, searchable catalog that includes 8,077 that are easy to copy, revise, remix, and redistribute using Pressbooks' publishing platform.
  • Open Stax run by Rice University is another source of free textbooks using Creative Commons licenses. Most can also be found on the Open Textbook Library.
  • B.C. Open Collection by BC Campus aggregator of textbooks
  • LibreTexts adaptable, user-friendly non-profit open education resource platform for creating, customizing, and sharing accessible, interactive textbooks, adaptive homework, and ancillary materials.
  • Algorithms, 4th ed. by Robert Sedgewick & Kevin Wayne - online, abbreviated version of this classic text. Goes with the Algorithms I & II courses offered through Coursera and Cubits.
  • Software Engineering: A Modern Approach by Marco Tulio Valente online textbook for undergraduate software engineering courses.

Open textbooks can also be found on some courseware sites.

Curricula

  • CodeHS has over 100 customizable courses, a built in LMS, and an online IDE that supports 10+ languages including JavaScript, Java, HTML, Python, C++, C#, & React Native.
  • BWagner, Computer Science curricula for International Baccalaureate (IB) classes.
  • Canvas Commons is a free service. Content can be downloaded or imported directly into a Canvas course shell by a teacher Canvas account. Educators can upload content, too.
  • Coursera founded by Stanford and Princeton, has free content, paid content, and paid content that can be audited for free.
  • EdX was founded by MIT and Harvard. It is a paid service, but some courses can be audited for free.
  • MIT Open Courseware is a free service containing content from nearly all MIT classes
  • Cubits is a paid server with primarily computer science content with emphasis on Algorithms and AI. Cubits was founded by Princeton Professor Robert Sedgewick and contains mostly his content. Most of the courses can also be found on Coursera.
  • OER Commons is a public digital library of textbooks and courseware
  • Concordia OER directory to search for digital resources
  • The Ontario Curriculum on Computer Studies published by the Ontario Ministry of Education for secondary education.
  • Microsoft Learn for technical skills, training, and certifications.
  • Teachers Pay Teachers Sharing site for teaching resources.
  • Code.org offers a wide range of pre-built computer science course curricula, including for AP CSP and CSA. Comes with an online IDE, lesson plans, worksheets, videos, exercises and projects. Also includes lesson plans and materials for offline lessons which is something we have not seen from other similar organizations.
  • Carnegie Mellon University CS Academy offers a smaller range of curricula but their flagship program is a great curriculum for an introductory class. This one is good at breaking things into small chunks, students complete a lot of small projects gradually building skills and knowledge. Very low friction, low barrier to entry. Also comes with an online IDE and the ability to monitor student progress.
  • Khan Academy Computing lessons in Python, Javascript, Algorithms, Computer Science, and more.
  • OSPI Suggested Computer Science Resources - selection of suggested supplemental Computer Science Resources by the Washington Office of Superintendent of Public Instruction.
  • Saylor.org Academy - free online courses
  • Illinois Computer Science Teacher Toolkit computer science resources, discussion forums, information on STEM-related events and job postings
