Formative Assessments

Having good formative assessments is a crucial component of any classroom. With formative assessments the emphasis is on giving students quick, timely, actionable feedback. Good formative assessments are not necessarily about giving a grade but helping students learn.

Peer Review

When designing a project build in a peer review component. Peer review helps students by not only facilitating them receiving feedback but also by putting them in a position to give feedback. Having to give someone else feedback forces you to think critically about the assignment and may help students look at their own work in a new light. Students may need guidance on how to give feedback so it is a good idea to structure this activity by using a worksheet or form that guides students on what to look for when evaluating peer work.

Circulation

When students are working on projects the teacher can accomplish a lot by circulating around the classroom, observing students, and offering feedback where needed. Try to look out for students who are stuck, struggling, or taking the wrong approach and give them feedback to help them find their way to the correct path. This way you can give students quick, frequent feedback even if they are working on a long form project.

Entry Tasks and Exit Tickets

Entry tasks and exit tickets are a good practice in general for setting up a strong classroom routine. They also give you an opportunity for a quick formative assessment at the beginning or end of a class. For entry tasks ask a simple review question from a previous lesson. Exit tasks let you reflect on what was learned in the lesson that day. For both it is important to make these quick for students and quick for you to assess as well.

Rubrics

Any project you have the students work on should include a rubric. Rubrics help students assess their own work and understand what they need to include. Rubrics can include sections on the coding concepts you want the students to focus on, good coding practices you want students to follow, and anything else you want to empahsize.
